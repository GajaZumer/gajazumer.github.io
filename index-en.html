GAJA ŽUMER

After completing the first level of the University study program Mathematics at the Faculty of Mathematics and Physics in Ljubljana, I continued my studies at the second level at the Faculty of Electrical Engineering (Department of Robotics). During my studies, I worked for half a year at Revoz in the robotics department, as an applicator and manager I participated in the traditional days of industrial robotics and became interested in for technologies of extended reality (XR), from which I also got a master's degree.
After my studies, I got a job at the SiEVA research center as the head of the newly established Laboratory for Digital Technologies. The goal of the laboratory is to encourage and help companies from the automotive industry to implement XR technologies into their production and work processes. We act as a hub, connecting companies from automotive industry, faculties and suppliers of hardware and software and popularize and strengthen knowledge in all three mentioned verticals.

PORTFOLIO


Master's thesis on Virtual Reality in the automotive industry

The Master's thesis is in two parts. The first part provides a comprehensive overview of VR with a focus on the automotive industry, while the second part summarises the practical development of three VR applications covering different elements of the manufacturing and business process. Demo applications are developed in the Unity game engine and are compatible with Valve Index VR glasses:

fire safety training application (inspired by the built-in force sensors in the Valve Index controllers),


VR simulation of production process and training of workers, where user must insert ball bearings into a U-profile using a hand press,



application for creating and simulating new workstations to simplify ergonomics assessment to facilitate the work of design engineers.



Organiser and mentor at the 5-day workshop "Virtualno=zabavno" as part of the Summer Camp of Innovative Technologies at the Faculty of Electrical Engineering

In 2021 and 2022, I organised and carried out a 5-day workshop for elementary and high-school students on extended reality and XR game programming. Through lectures, testing several types of smart XR hardware (Oculus Quest 2, Valve Index, Hololens 2, etc.) and practical project work on the computer, participants learned:

the basics of XR and 360° technology,

using Vuforia SDK.

More information on the content of the workshops can be found under the tab WORKSHOPS.

Blood rush - infinite runner

The project is a work in progress. It is a prototype for infinite runner games. The player has to navigate a blood cell inside a blood vessel and collect oxygen. Each oxygen particle gives player an opportunity to dash and eliminate any blood clots that are in the way. If a player hits a blood clot without dashing, the game is over. I have implemented a heartbeat that accelerates the moveemnt of a blood vessel at regular intervals and a laminar flow that is stronger towards the centre of the blood vessel and slower near the vesscular wall.
The final application is available here.

2D side scroller

The project is a work in progress. It is a prototype for side scroller games. You play as an anomaly exterminator near mysterious lighthouse. There are several types of anomalies; in the current version of the game there is a teleportation anomaly (which prevents you from leaving) and image distortion anomaly. Short video is showing main functionalities and game components:

main menu and pause menu (Resume, Quit, New Game, Load Game, Settings (change volume and set language), Return to main menu),
the script and monologues/thoughts are written in Twine (monologues are written in Slovene and English for localisation purposes),
implementation of Twine output to Unity,
inventory system (working UI, equip and use function),
dialogue system (Twine document is converted into an array of strings (sentences in Slovene or English) to be displayed as a text object triggered by a specific event),
grab-pass shader (used for distortion anomaly) and water shader (used in main menu),
saving in loading game and settings info (.json file is storing data on background music volume, sound effects volume, player position, state of the inventory),
drawing and implementation of infinite background (fog),
background and some inventory items hand-drawn in Krita.
